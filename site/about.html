About

This is a transparent recombination engine. It generates new sentences by mechanically splicing fragments from a curated library of public-domain texts — natural history field notes, personal letters, philosophy, trial transcripts, ship logs, folk tales, scientific papers, and poetry. Every sentence on this site carries a complete record of exactly which source files, and which character spans within those files, contributed each fragment. Click any sentence to see the receipts. No neural networks are involved in the generation; the engine is a plain Python script that anyone can read, run, and audit.

The corpus, the generation engine, the provenance records, and this site are all open source. The project runs automatically: a GitHub Actions workflow generates a new batch of sentences each day, commits the provenance JSON, and the site updates itself.

Three corpus texts — Darwin's Voyage of the Beagle, Frazer's Golden Bough, and Marco Polo's Travels — are stored as partial files, trimmed to keep the total corpus under 10 MB. The corpus_index.json records a "truncated": true flag on each. The complete originals are available at the source URLs listed in that file.
